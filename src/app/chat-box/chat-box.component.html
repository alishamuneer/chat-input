Switzerland's national elections refer to the process through which Swiss citizens elect their federal representatives and government officials.1 Switzerland has a unique political system characterized by direct democracy, federalism, and a multi-party system. Here's a summary of how national elections work in Switzerland:2

  • Federal Structure: Switzerland is a federal state, consisting of 26 cantons, each with significant autonomy in various policy areas. The federal government is responsible for foreign affairs, defense, and other national-level functions.3
  • Bicameral Legislature: The Swiss Federal Assembly is the country's legislature, comprising two chambers: the National Council (lower house) and the Council of States (upper house). The National Council has 200 members elected through a proportional representation system, while the Council of States has 46 members, with each canton sending two representatives.
